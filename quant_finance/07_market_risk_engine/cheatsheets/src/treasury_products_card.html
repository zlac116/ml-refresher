Master skeleton: V = (market_implied − contract) · scale · discount

◆ Master Recipe (4 steps)

  1. List cashflows — when, ccy, contingent on what
  2. Substitute each random CF with risk-neutral expectation (↓)
  3. Discount by DF(t) (or DF(t,s) for credit)
  4. Sum: V = Σ E[CF_t]·DF(t)

◆ Universal Patterns

A — Linear-in-strike

V = (market − contract) · scale · disc
Par strike = market-implied (set V=0).
Used by: FX fwd, FRA, ZCIS, IRS, CDS

B — Basis × annuity

V = b · N · A(T) [· FX]
Used by: Sec lending, tenor basis, XCCY basis

C — Cashflow strip PV

V = ± Σ cf_i · uplift_i · DF(t_i, s)
Used by: Bond, FB, Linker, Loan, Annuity Liab
Recognise the pattern → recall the formula. Each product is one of these three shapes.

◆ Walk-through: FRA via recipe

1. CF: N(L−K)τ at T₂ 2. L → (D₁/D₂−1)/τ = fwd 3. discount: · DF(T₂) 4. V = N(fwd−K)τ·DF(T₂)

◆ Portfolio Recipe (ALM)

  1. Exposure vector — bucket by KRD (2y/5y/10y/30y), IE01, CS01, FX Δ, vega
  2. Aggregate signed contributions (assets +, liabs −)
  3. Hedging set — IRS strip + Linker strip + nominal bonds + OIS + ZCIS
  4. Solve linear system → hedge notionals; report residuals
Parallel DV01 ≠ KRD. A 10y liab hedged with 5y IRS is parallel-neutral but long-end exposed. KRD-mismatch = canonical pension failure mode.

◆ Convention reminders

  • bp → decimal: / 1e4. 15bp = 0.0015.
  • Inflation compounds: (1+b)^t (NOT 1+b·t).
  • τ must match contract day-count.
  • Annuity liability is NEGATIVE.
  • FRA fwd: SIMPLE (D₁/D₂−1)/τ, not continuous.
  • Swap float telescope: 1 − DF(T) (single-curve).
  • CDS hazard: λ ≈ s/(1−R); Q(t)=exp(−λt).
  • Black option vol: lognormal in F, not S.
  • Multi-curve (post-2008): OIS discount + LIBOR/SOFR project — drills ignore.

● I — Cash & Money Market

FX Spot (§1)
V = N_for · S · fx_factor
Own foreign currency. Pure FX delta; no rate or time.
Risk: FX
Repo / Reverse Repo (§2)
V = N(1 + r·T)·DF(T) − N
Secured short-term borrow (repo) / lend (reverse). V≈0 at par; small DV01.
Risk: rate (small)
Sec Lending (§3)
V = (fee_bp/1e4) · N · A(T)
Lend securities; receive fee per period. Pattern B.
Risk: rate (tiny)
Amortising Loan (§4, linear amort)
V = Σ cf_i · DF(i, spread_bp) amort = P/n, cf_i = amort + c·outstanding
Equal principal/period + interest on remainder. For annuity-style (equal total pmt) use pmt = P·c/(1−(1+c)^−n).
Risk: rate, credit spread (CS01)
E1 T-Bill / discount paper
V = face · DF(T) (zero coupon, govt) dy = (face−P)/face · 360/days
Risk: rate
E2 Commercial Paper
V = face · DF(T, spread_bp)
Short corp/bank discount paper.
Risk: rate, credit

▲ II — Forwards

FX Forward (§5)
V = N_for · (S·fx_factor − K) · DF(T)
Locked-rate currency exchange at T. K_par = S·DF_f/DF_d (IRP).
Risk: FX, rate
FRA (§6)
fwd = (DF(T₁)/DF(T₂) − 1)/τ V = N·(fwd − K)·τ·DF(T₂)
Locked rate on future deposit [T₁,T₂]. K_par = fwd.
Risk: rate
MTM uses curve-implied fwd; real settle = N(L−K)τ/(1+L·τ) paid at T₁.

■ III — Bonds (Pattern C — cashflow strips)

Vanilla Bond (§7)
V = Σ cf_i · DF(t_i, spread_bp)
Coupons + principal, discounted at issuer's credit curve.
Risk: rate (DV01), credit (CS01)
Foreign Bond (§8)
V = (Σ cf_i · DF(t_i, s_bp)) · S · fx_factor
Bond in foreign currency, translated at spot.
Risk: rate, credit, FX
Linker (inflation-linked) (§9)
V = Σ cf_real_i · (1+b)^t_i · DF(t_i)
CFs uplifted by inflation. Long inflation = b ↑ → V ↑.
Risk: rate, breakeven (IE01)
Indexation lag: 3M (Gilts) / 8M (TIPS) — adds small IE01 basis.
E3 Zero-Coupon Bond (long)
V = face · DF(T, s) D_mac = T; D_mod = T / (1 + y/m)
No coupons; max duration per $ of face.
E4 Floating-Rate Note (FRN) (at reset)
V (at fixing) ≈ face·spread·A + face
Resets to ~par; duration ≈ time-to-next-fixing. Between resets the locked coupon adds accrual.
Risk: spread (dominant), tiny rate
E5 Callable Bond
V = V_straight − V_call_option
Issuer's right to redeem early → bondholder bears asymmetric risk; negative convexity.
Risk: rate, credit, vol
E6 Convertible Bond
V = V_straight + V_equity_call
Bond + long call on issuer's equity. Multi-factor.
Risk: rate, credit, equity, equity vol

◆ IV — Swaps

Interest Rate Swap (IRS) (§10)
V_rec = N·[c·A(T) − (1 − DF(T))] where A(T) = Σ τ·DF(t_i) c_par = (1 − DF(T)) / A(T)
Exchange fixed-c vs floating; receive-fix sign shown. Pay-fix flips sign.
Risk: rate (THE rates instrument)
ZCIS Inflation Swap (§11)
V = N · DF(T) · [(1+b)^T − (1+K)^T] K_par = b
Single payment at T: inflation index vs fixed.
Risk: rate, breakeven
Tenor Basis Swap (§12)
V = b · N · A(T) b = (basis_bp + shock_bp)/1e4
e.g. 3M vs 6M floating spread. Pattern B.
Risk: rate, tenor-basis
XCCY Basis Swap (§13)
V = N_for · S · fx_factor · b · A(T)
Cross-currency basis leg in foreign ccy; FX sensitive.
Risk: rate, xccy-basis, FX
Real XCCY: principal exchange + MtM resets (drill omits).
Total Return Swap (TRS) (§14)
V = PV(reference asset) − funding_notional
Synthetic long the reference (balance-sheet light).
Risk: inherits reference's risks
E7 Overnight Index Swap (OIS)
Same as IRS; float leg = compounded O/N
Risk-free swap; benchmark discount curve post-2008.
E8 CMS
float leg = swap rate (e.g. 10y) → convexity adj. needed (SABR surface dependent)
Bet on curve shape (steepening/flattening).
E9 Asset Swap (ASW)
Bond + offsetting IRS → synthetic floater ASW spread ≈ bond coupon − par swap rate
Isolates credit spread, neutralises rate.
E10 YoY Inflation Swap
Each year: N·(π_i − K)·τ (vs ZCIS = single compounded payment)
Hedge year-by-year inflation; convexity adj vs ZCIS.

▲ V — Credit

Credit Default Swap (CDS) (§15)
λ = market_spread / (1−R) Q(t) = exp(−λ·t) premium = Σ τ·DF·Q protect = Σ (Q_prev − Q)·DF V_buyer = N·[protect·(1−R) − premium·K]
Buyer pays K, receives (1−R)·N on default. Buyer wins when spread widens. K_par = market_spread.
Risk: credit spread (dominant), rate (small)
E13 CDS Index (CDX/iTraxx)
Same as §15. Each defaulting name pays (1/n)·(1−R)·N. Remaining notional continues to accrue premium.
CDX IG = 125 US IG names @ 100bp; HY @ 500bp. NOT a first-to-default basket.
Risk: spread (CS01), correlation (tranches)
E14 MBS — agency pass-through
V = Σ E[CF_t | prepay_model] · DF(t, OAS) (MC over rate paths + CPR/PSA prepayments)
Negative convexity from prepayment option.
Risk: rate (effective DV01), prepay, OAS, vol

● VI — Options

FX Option (§16)
F = S·fx_factor / DF(T) d1 = [ln(F/K) + ½v²T] / (v√T); d2 = d1 − v√T V_call = N·DF(T)·[F·N(d1) − K·N(d2)] V_put = N·DF(T)·[K·N(−d2) − F·N(−d1)]
Right to exchange currencies at K on T.
Risk: FX (delta), vol (vega), rate
Swaption (§17)
A = Σ τ·DF(t_i) (swap annuity) S = [DF(T_exp) − DF(T_end)] / A (fwd swap rate) d1, d2 as Black on S V_payer = N·A·[S·N(d1) − K·N(d2)] V_recv = N·A·[K·N(−d2) − S·N(−d1)]
Option to enter IRS at K. Black model on forward swap rate.
Risk: rate, vol (vega dominates at ATM)
E11 Cap / Floor / Caplet
Caplet at t_i: τ·N·max(L_i − K, 0) paid at t_{i+1} Cap = Σ caplets, each Black on fwd rate
Hedge floating-rate exposure. Pure vol play.
E12 Bermudan Swaption
Multiple exercise dates → LSMC / lattice V > European by "early exercise premium"
Used for callable bond hedging.
Risk: rate, vol, mean reversion (model dep.)
E15 Variance Swap
Payoff = N_var · (σ²_real − K²) N_vega = 2K · N_var (approx near K) Replication: OTM calls + puts (1/K² weights)
Pure realised-vol exposure; tail hedge.
E16 Equity TRS / E17 Commodity Fwd
Equity TRS: V = PV(equity total return) − funding Commodity Fwd: V = N·(F_T − K)·DF(T)
Synthetic equity / hedge raw materials.

■ VII — Liabilities

Annuity Liability (§18)
V = − payment · Σ (1+b)^t · DF(t), t=1..years
Pension-style: you OWE inflation-linked payments. NEGATIVE PV.
Risk: rate (rates ↓ → V more neg), breakeven (b ↑ → V more neg).
Hedge: Linker matches IE01 partly; IRS matches DV01.
Sign trap: liability has opposite-sign DV01 to a long bond. Pick one DV01 convention across your stack. KRD-mismatched hedges (parallel-neutral, long-end-exposed) are the canonical pension hedge failure.

◆ VIII — Risk Metrics

Macaulay Duration (years)
D_mac = Σ t_i·PV_i / Σ PV_i
PV-weighted average time to cashflows.
Modified Duration (%)
D_mod = D_mac / (1 + y/m) ΔP/P ≈ −D_mod · Δy
DV01 / PV01 ($)
DV01 = D_mod · P · 0.0001
$ P&L per 1 bp rate move.
Convexity
C = Σ t_i(t_i+1/m)·PV_i / [P·(1+y/m)²] ΔP/P ≈ −D_mod·Δy + ½·C·Δy²
Positive for long vanilla bonds → cushions losses, boosts gains.
Greeks (Black on forward F)
Δ deltaDF(T)·N(d1)
Γ gammaDF(T)·φ(d1)/(F·σ·√T)
ν vegaDF(T)·F·φ(d1)·√T
Θ theta− for long opt (time decay)
ρ rhocall-rho > 0
Gamma uses F, not S, in the denominator.
Numerical (FD) when no closed-form
DV01_fd = [V(+1bp) − V(−1bp)] / 2 Conv_fd = [V(+h) − 2V + V(−h)] / (h²·V)

◆ Top ALM caveats (full list in gold-ref PDF)

  • Multi-curve: OIS discount + LIBOR/SOFR projection (drills: single-curve).
  • LDI regimes (UK FRS102 / US IAS 19): liab discounted on AA-corp → swap-vs-AA basis is unhedged.
  • LPI caps: UK pension inflation capped at 5% → liability non-linear; ZCIS doesn't hedge convexity.
  • Sept 2022 gilt crisis: LDI hedge collateral calls cascaded — the hedge itself has liquidity risk.
  • Fisher: nominal ≈ real + breakeven. Linker DV01 = REAL-rate sensitivity, not nominal.
